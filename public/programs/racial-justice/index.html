Racial Justice

The Racial Justice Program aims to preserve and extend constitutionally guaranteed rights to people who have historically been denied their rights on the basis of race. We pursue actions to transform our community that affirms the value of people of color and the disenfranchised.

Between 1980 and 2015, the number of people incarcerated in America increased from roughly 500,000 to over 2.2 million.

The imprisonment rate for African American women is twice that of white women.

A criminal record can reduce the likelihood of a callback or job offer by nearly 50 percent. The negative impact of a criminal record is twice as large for African American applicants.

Human Trafficking

We’re committed in the fight against human trafficking, exploitation and modern-day slavery through partnerships with local organizations and programs. For potential victims and survivors of human trafficking, we seek to provide help and resources available to them.

Human Trafficking

What

Human trafficking is the action or practice of illegally transporting people from one country or area to another, typically for the purposes of forced labour or commercial sexual exploitation. It is the third largest international crime industry (behind illegal drugs and arms trafficking).

How

Trafficking victims are often lured into another country by false promises. All the work is done under the menace of a penalty or the person has not offered himself voluntarily and is now unable to leave.

Why

The person is recruited and forced or deceived into conducting some form of criminal activity such as pick pocketing, begging, benefit fraud, and prostitution. Through corecion, a person will experience a serious form of denial of freedom.

It Exists in LA

California harbors three of the FBI’s 13 highest child sex trafficking areas in the nation: Los Angeles, San Francisco and San Diego.
